Dr. Norman Borlaug

The father of the green revolution.

Dr. Norman Borlaug managed to create a high-yield hybrid wheat that was at the same time resistant to parasites and insensitive to daylight hours, allowing it to be grown in a variety of climates.

About Dr. Norman Borlaug's work

  • Borned in March 25 of 1914, Norman Ernest Borlaug was an American agronomist who studied plant pathology and genetics at the University of Minnesota between 1937 and 1942;
  • His research focused primarily in the development of semi-dwarf, high-yield, and disease -resistant wheat varieties;
  • Combined with modern agricultural prodution techniques, the wheat varieties produced by Borlaug were introduced (by himself) along the mid-20th century in Mexico, greatly improving its production and exportation by the country by 1963;
  • With the same methodoloy applied in Mexico, Pakistan's and India's wheat yields nearly doubled between 1965 and 1970, improving considerably the food security of those nations.

    • Dr. Norman Borlaug's Prizes

  • Nobel Peace Prize (1970): "for his contributions to the 'green revolution' that was having such an impact on food production particularly in Asia and in Latin America."
  • American Academy of Achievement's Golden Plate Award (1971)
  • Peace Medal (1974)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (1977)
  • S. Roger Horchow Award for Greatest Public Service by a Private Citizen (1980)
  • Public Welfare Medal (2002)
  • Rotary International Award for World Understanding and Peace (2002)
  • National Medal of Science (2004)
